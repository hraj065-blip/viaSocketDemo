FAQ

Questions we hear
every day.

What's the difference between platform plans and service plans?
+
Platform plans give you access to the viaSocket tool — you build and manage your own workflows. Service plans (Guided Setup & Managed) mean our team does the building and management for you. Most service clients also have a platform plan — the services run on top of the platform.
Can I switch between tiers at any time?
+
Yes. Platform plans can be upgraded or downgraded at any time. If you downgrade, you keep access until the end of your billing period. For service plans, Guided Setup is a one-time engagement with no lock-in. Managed plans have a 3-month minimum commitment.
What does the 99.9% SLA actually mean?
+
99.9% uptime means your workflows can experience at most 8.76 hours of downtime per year. This is contractual on Growth and Business plans — if we miss it, you receive service credits automatically. We publish a live status page and have redundant infrastructure to back this commitment.
How does the Guided Setup service work in practice?
+
After payment, you're booked for a 2-hour discovery call. We map your processes, identify automation opportunities, and scope 2 workflows. Our engineer builds and tests them (typically 3–5 working days), then we run a training session and hand over full documentation. 30-day support follows.
Do I need technical knowledge for any plan?
+
For Self-Serve plans — basic comfort with apps and logic helps, but our AI builder minimises the learning curve significantly. For Guided Setup and Managed — zero technical knowledge required. You describe your business process in plain language; we handle everything else.
What happens if my workflow breaks on a Managed plan?
+
Our monitoring system detects failures in real time. For Managed clients, our team is alerted automatically and begins remediation within 4 hours — often before you notice anything is wrong. For Self-Serve, you receive email alerts and can troubleshoot using our built-in debugger and support team.
